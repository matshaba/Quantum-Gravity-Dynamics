\section{Overview}

This chapter derives the QGD predictions for binary merger ringdown, gravitational
wave energy and flux, and black hole thermodynamics. The key structural result is the
mode decomposition of the $\sigma$-field:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Spin-2 tensor modes}: massless, propagate at speed $c$, carry the
    observable gravitational wave energy. These reproduce the GR quadrupole formula
    exactly.
  \item \textbf{Spin-0 scalar mode}: massive (Planck-scale), Yukawa-suppressed over
    any macroscopic distance. Locally real near the binary; globally unobservable.
  \item \textbf{Spin-1 vector mode}: subject to constraint equations; does not contribute
    to TT radiation.
\end{itemize}

This mode decomposition (established in Chapter~13) underlies all radiation predictions
in this chapter. The Pais--Uhlenbeck factored form of the master field equation,
\begin{equation}
  \Box_g(\Box_g - \mQ^2)\sigma_\mu = -\mQ^2 S_\mu,
  \quad \mQ = \frac{M_\mathrm{Pl}}{\sqrt\kappa} \approx 0.71\,M_\mathrm{Pl},
\end{equation}
splits the propagator into a massless pole ($1/k^2$, long-range) and a massive pole
($-1/(k^2 + \mQ^2)$, Planck-scale Yukawa). Observable gravitational radiation comes
entirely from the massless pole.

\section{Quasi-Normal Mode Spectrum}

\subsection{Agreement with GR at Leading Order}

The perturbation equation on the Kerr background:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\Box_g\delta\sigma - \kappa\lQ^2\Box_g^2\delta\sigma = 0.}
\end{equation}
At leading order, the stiffness ratio $\kappa\lQ^2/r_s^2 \sim 10^{-140}$, so the
equation reduces exactly to the standard Teukolsky equation.
\textbf{QGD quasi-normal modes equal GR at leading order.}

\subsection{Dominant 220 Mode}

The dominant ringdown mode (Echeverr\'ia--Leaver):
\begin{align}
  \omega_{220} &= \frac{c}{2GM_f}\left[1 - 0.63(1-\chi_f)^{0.3}\right], \\
  \tau_{220} &= \frac{2GM_f}{c^3}\left[1 - 0.63(1-\chi_f)^{0.45}\right]^{-1},
\end{align}
where $M_f$ and $\chi_f$ are the final mass and dimensionless spin.

\subsection{Quantum Stiffness Correction}

The leading QGD correction to the QNM frequency:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\delta\omega}{\omega} \sim \kappa\lQ^2\omega^2 \sim \left(\frac{\lQ}{r_s}\right)^2
  \approx 10^{-140} \quad \text{(stellar-mass BH).}
\end{equation}
This is below any conceivable measurement threshold.

\section{Complete Ringdown Waveform}

The full QGD ringdown waveform has two observable components:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{h_\mathrm{QGD}(t) = h_\mathrm{QNM}(t) + h_\mathrm{cross}(t).}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Standard QNM Component}

$h_\mathrm{QNM}(t)$ is the sum over quasi-normal modes, identical to the GR result
at leading order in $\lQ/r_s$.

\subsection{Cross-Term Decay}

The cross-term $2\sigma_t^{(1)}\sigma_t^{(2)}$ in $g_{tt}$ carries information
about the pre-merger mass ratio. It contributes a distinct transient signal:
\begin{equation}
  h_\mathrm{cross}(t) = A_\mathrm{cross}\cdot 2\sigma_t^{(1)}\sigma_t^{(2)}
    \exp(-t/\tau_\mathrm{cross}),
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\tau_\mathrm{cross} = \frac{6GM_\mathrm{tot}}{c^3}
    \approx 6\times10^{-5}\;\mathrm{s}\times\frac{M_\mathrm{tot}}{\Msun}.}
\end{equation}
This signal has zero oscillation frequency (it is a monotonic decay) and precedes
the clean quasi-normal mode phase.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Prediction P1}: every binary merger, regardless of mass ratio, produces
this cross-term transient before the QNM ringdown.

\medskip
\noindent The spin-0 sector does \emph{not} contribute to the ringdown signal for the
same reason it does not contribute to inspiral radiation: the massive mode is
Yukawa-suppressed below any astrophysical detection threshold (Chapter~13, Section~4).

\section{Gravitational Wave Energy and the Quadrupole Formula}

\subsection{Noether Energy-Momentum Tensor}

QGD provides a coordinate-independent, locally conserved gravitational energy tensor:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{T^{\mu\nu}_\mathrm{QGD} = \nabla^\mu\sigma_\alpha\nabla^\nu\sigma^\alpha
    - \frac{1}{2}g^{\mu\nu}(\nabla\sigma)^2 + \lQ^2\text{-corrections}.}
\end{equation}
This is a \emph{true tensor} (not a pseudotensor), positive-definite:
\begin{equation}
  H[\sigma,\pi] = \int d^3x\left[\frac{1}{2}\pi_\mu\pi^\mu
    + \frac{1}{2}(\nabla\sigma_\mu)^2 + V(\sigma)\right] \geq 0,
\end{equation}
with equality only for flat Minkowski. The gravitational wave energy density is exact,
without the averaging over several wavelengths required in GR:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\rho_\mathrm{GW} = \frac{1}{2}\omega^2|\epsilon|^2.}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Gravitational Wave Flux}

The power radiated by the spin-2 tensor modes:
\begin{equation}
  P = \frac{c^3}{8\pi G}\int d\Omega\,r^2\langle\dot{h}_{ij}^{TT}\dot{h}_{ij}^{TT}\rangle,
\end{equation}
where $h_{ij}^{TT}$ is the transverse-traceless metric perturbation. For a binary
in the wave zone:
\begin{equation}
  h_{ij}^{TT}(t,\mathbf{x}) = \frac{2G}{c^4 r}\,\Lambda_{ij,kl}(\hat{n})\,
    \ddot{Q}_{kl}(t-r/c),
\end{equation}
with $Q_{kl} = \sum_a M_a(x_a^k x_a^l - \tfrac{1}{3}\delta^{kl}r_a^2)$ the standard
mass quadrupole. Integrating over solid angles yields the \textbf{quadrupole formula}:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{P = \frac{G}{5c^5}\left\langle\dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}^{ij}
    - \frac{1}{3}(\dddot{Q}_{kk})^2\right\rangle
    = \frac{32}{5}\frac{G^4}{c^5}\frac{M_1^2M_2^2 M}{r^5}\cdot f(e).}
\end{equation}
This is the complete expression for the observable gravitational radiation from a
binary at all astrophysical separations.

\subsection{Gravitational Poynting Vector}

Energy flux is locally defined at any radius:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\mathbf{S}_\mathrm{grav} = \dot\sigma_\mu\nabla\sigma^\mu,
    \qquad \frac{dE}{dt} = \oint_S \mathbf{S}_\mathrm{grav}\cdot d\mathbf{A}.}
\end{equation}

\section{The $\sigma$-Field Near-Field Structure}

\subsection{The Cross-Term $\sigma_1\sigma_2$}

In the binary's near zone, the product $\sigma_t^{(1)}\sigma_t^{(2)}$ enters the
metric cross-term. Its multipole expansion:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_1\sigma_2 \approx \frac{2G\sqrt{M_1M_2}}{c^2 r}
  \left[1 + \frac{\mathbf{x}\cdot\bigl(\mathbf{r}_1+\mathbf{r}_2\bigr)}{2r^2}
    + \mathcal{O}(r^{-2})\right],
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{r}_1+\mathbf{r}_2 = \frac{M_1-M_2}{M}\,\mathbf{r}_{12}$ (non-zero
for unequal masses).

The dipole component of $\sigma_1\sigma_2$ sources only the massive spin-0 mode,
whose propagator is:
\begin{equation}
  D(k^2) = \frac{1}{k^2} - \frac{1}{k^2 + \mQ^2}.
\end{equation}
At orbital frequencies $\omega\ll \mQ c^2/\hbar$, the second term dominates and
gives a non-propagating, quasi-static Yukawa response. The corresponding
suppression at any astrophysical distance $r$ is:
\begin{equation}
  e^{-\mQ r/\hbar} \sim e^{-r/\lP}.
\end{equation}
For the Hulse-Taylor binary at orbital separation $a \approx 1.95\times10^9\,\mathrm{m}$:
\begin{equation}
  e^{-a/\lP} \approx 10^{-5.2\times10^{43}},
\end{equation}
an absolute suppression rendering any spin-0 radiation completely unobservable.

\subsection{The $D$-Factor}

The dipole asymmetry factor $D = (\sqrt{M_1}-\sqrt{M_2})^2/M$ measures the amplitude
difference between the two bodies' $\sigma$-fields. It characterises the near-field
$\sigma$-energy redistribution between the bodies over the orbital timescale.
It does not enter the observable gravitational wave power or waveform phase.
$D = 0$ when $M_1 = M_2$ (the symmetric case, a null test).

\section{Black Hole Thermodynamics}

\subsection{Hawking Temperature}

The Hawking temperature follows from the Taylor expansion of the QGD phase factor
around the horizon, without invoking quantum field theory in curved spacetime:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{T_H = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi GMk_B}.}
\end{equation}
Verification: $\kappa = \tfrac{1}{2}c^2|d(\sigma^2)/dr|_{\sigma=1} = c^4/(4GM)$,
giving $T_H = \hbar\kappa/(2\pi c k_B)$. Relative error $< 3\times10^{-11}$.

\subsection{Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy}

Integrating $dS = dE/T_H$ from infinity to mass $M$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{S_\mathrm{BH} = \frac{k_B c^3 A}{4G\hbar} = \frac{k_B A}{4\lP^2}.}
\end{equation}
The $\sigma$-field mode count gives $S_\mathrm{modes} = (\ln 2/\pi)S_\mathrm{BH}
\approx 0.22\,S_\mathrm{BH}$, an $\mathcal{O}(1)$ pre-factor ambiguity from the
UV cutoff prescription.

\subsection{Kerr Horizon Condition}

At the outer Kerr horizon $r_+$ where $\Delta = r^2 - r_s r + a^2 = 0$:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_t^2 - \sigma_J^2 = 1, \quad
  \sigma_J = \frac{a}{r_+}.
\end{equation}
Proof: $r_s r_+ = r_+^2 + a^2$ implies $\sigma_t^2 - \sigma_J^2 = (r_s r_+ - a^2)/r_+^2 = 1$.
Verified for $a^* \in [0,\,0.999]$.

Universal pattern for Kerr-Newman:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_t^2 + \sigma_Q^2 - \sigma_J^2 = 1
  \quad \text{(charge adds, spin subtracts).}
\end{equation}
