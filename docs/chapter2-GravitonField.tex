\section{From the Wavefunction to the Graviton Field}
\label{sec:origin}
%=============================================================

The preceding sections established that the complete QGD wavefunction for a
gravitational system is the four-component Dirac spinor
\begin{equation}
  \psi = \frac{\alpha_G}{r}\!\left[
    \psi_0\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\\sqrt{f}\\ia\sin\theta\sqrt{g}\end{pmatrix}e^{-iS/\hbar}
    + \psi_1\begin{pmatrix}0\\1\\-ia\sin\theta\sqrt{g}\\-\sqrt{f}\end{pmatrix}e^{-iS/\hbar}
    + \psi_2\begin{pmatrix}\sqrt{f}\\ia\sin\theta\sqrt{g}\\1\\0\end{pmatrix}e^{+iS/\hbar}
    + \psi_3\begin{pmatrix}-ia\sin\theta\sqrt{g}\\-\sqrt{f}\\0\\1\end{pmatrix}e^{+iS/\hbar}
  \right]
  \label{eq:complete_wavefunction}
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_G = e^{i\pi/4}\sqrt{c\hbar/(2GMm)}$ is the gravitational fine
structure constant, $S(x)$ is the Hamilton--Jacobi phase, and $f(r,\theta)$ is
the \textbf{universal gravitational scalar}:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{f(r,\theta) = \frac{2GM}{c^2 r}
    - \frac{GQ^2}{c^4 r^2}
    + \frac{2Mr}{\Sigma}
    + \frac{\Lambda r^2}{3}
    + \frac{b(r)}{r}
    + H^2(t)\,r^2
    - \int\!\frac{P(r)}{\rho(r)c^2}\,\dd r
    + \kappa\frac{\hbar^2}{M^2 c^2 r^2}}
  \label{eq:universal_f}
\end{equation}

Every known spacetime is a special case of $f$: Schwarzschild retains only the
first term, Reissner--Nordstr\"om adds the second, Kerr the third, and so on.
The lower spinor components contain $\sqrt{f}$, which encodes the
gravitational field strength.  This is the object we now promote to a field in
its own right.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Extracting the graviton field}

The wavefunction oscillates as $e^{\pm iS/\hbar}$.  In the semiclassical (WKB)
limit the phase $S(x)$ varies rapidly compared to the envelope, and its
gradient carries all dynamical information.  We define:

\begin{definition}[Graviton field]\label{def:graviton}
  The \textbf{graviton field} is the dimensionless four-vector
  \begin{equation}
    \boxed{\sigma_\mu(x) \;\equiv\; \frac{p_\mu}{mc} \;=\; \frac{1}{mc}\,\partial_\mu S(x)}
    \label{eq:sigma_def}
  \end{equation}
  where $p_\mu = \partial_\mu S$ is the canonical four-momentum from the
  Hamilton--Jacobi equation.
\end{definition}

Written as an explicit four-vector in the style of special relativity:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma^\mu = \left(\;\sigma_t,\;\sigma_x,\;\sigma_y,\;\sigma_z\;\right)
  = \frac{1}{mc}\left(\;\frac{E}{c},\;p_x,\;p_y,\;p_z\;\right)
  \label{eq:sigma_4vector}
\end{equation}

Each component counts the number of gravitational phase cycles per unit
coordinate length.  The temporal component $\sigma_t = E/(mc^2)$ counts
oscillations in time; the spatial components $\sigma_i = p_i/(mc)$ count
oscillations in space.

\begin{remark}[Connection to the wavefunction]
  The lower spinor components in Eq.~\eqref{eq:complete_wavefunction} are
  precisely the graviton field components:
  \begin{equation}
    \sqrt{f} = \frac{pc}{\Delta} = \sigma_z, \qquad
    ia\sin\theta\sqrt{g} = \frac{(p_x + ip_y)c}{\Delta} = \sigma_x + i\sigma_y
  \end{equation}
  where $\Delta = E + mc^2 + H$ is the energy denominator containing all
  stress-energy contributions.  The graviton field is not an
  independent postulate---it is \emph{read off} from the wavefunction.
\end{remark}

\section{The Master Metric}
\label{sec:master}
%=============================================================

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Statement}

The spacetime metric is constructed algebraically from the graviton fields:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{g_{\mu\nu}(x) = T^\alpha{}_\mu\;T^\beta{}_\nu
    \left(M_{\alpha\beta}\circ\left[
      \eta_{\alpha\beta}
      - \sum_{a=1}^{N}\varepsilon_a\,\sigma_\alpha^{(a)}\,\sigma_\beta^{(a)}
      - \kappa\,\lQ^2\;\partial_\alpha\sigma^\gamma\,\partial_\beta\sigma_\gamma
    \right]\right)}
  \label{eq:master}
\end{equation}

with $\eta_{\alpha\beta} = \mathrm{diag}(+1,-1,-1,-1)$.  We now explain each
element.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Components of the master equation}

\paragraph{The flat-space baseline $\eta_{\alpha\beta}$.}
The Minkowski metric provides the baseline.  When all graviton fields vanish
($\sigma = 0$), Eq.~\eqref{eq:master} reduces to the metric of special
relativity.

\paragraph{The graviton tensor $\sum\varepsilon_a\,\sigma_\alpha^{(a)}\sigma_\beta^{(a)}$.}
Each source contributes a rank-1 outer product $\sigma_\alpha\sigma_\beta$
weighted by its signature $\varepsilon_a$.  For a single mass source:
\begin{equation}
  g_{tt} = \eta_{tt} - \varepsilon\,\sigma_t^2 = +1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r}
  \qquad\checkmark
\end{equation}

For off-diagonal components (frame-dragging):
\begin{equation}
  g_{t\phi}^{(\mathrm{inner})} = \eta_{t\phi} - \varepsilon\,\sigma_t\,\sigma_\phi
  = 0 - \sigma_t\,\sigma_\phi
  = -\sigma_t\,\sigma_\phi \qquad\checkmark
\end{equation}

The minus sign on $g_{t\phi}$ (required for Kerr) emerges automatically from the
subtraction in $\eta - \varepsilon\,\sigma\sigma$.

\paragraph{The coordinate transformation $T^\alpha{}_\mu$.}
The matrix $T^\alpha{}_\mu$ maps from the fiducial Cartesian frame to physical
coordinates.  For spherical coordinates $(t,r,\theta,\phi)$:
\begin{equation}
  T^\alpha{}_\mu = \mathrm{diag}(1,\;1,\;r,\;r\sin\theta)
  \label{eq:T_spherical}
\end{equation}
encoding solid-angle geometry: $g_{\theta\theta} = r^2$,
$g_{\phi\phi} = r^2\sin^2\theta$.  For cosmological comoving coordinates with
scale factor $a(t)$:
\begin{equation}
  T^\alpha{}_\mu = \mathrm{diag}(1,\;a(t),\;a(t)\,r,\;a(t)\,r\sin\theta)
  \label{eq:T_cosmo}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{The geometric scaling matrix $M_{\alpha\beta}$.}
The Hadamard (element-wise) product with $M_{\alpha\beta}$ provides additional
geometric flexibility for specific coordinate systems.  For most applications
$M_{\alpha\beta} = \mathrm{diag}(1,1,1,1)$; in Kerr--Schild or isotropic
coordinates, nontrivial entries may be needed.

\paragraph{The quantum stiffness $\kappa\,\lQ^2\,\partial_\alpha\sigma^\gamma\partial_\beta\sigma_\gamma$.}
The gravitational analogue of an elastic stiffness modulus, where
\begin{equation}
  \lQ = \sqrt{\frac{G\hbar^2}{c^4}} \approx 1.6\times 10^{-70}\;\mathrm{m}
\end{equation}
is the quantum gravitational length scale and $\kappa \sim 2$ is fixed by the
$\sigma$-kinetic action.  This term is completely negligible at macroscopic
scales but prevents singularity formation at $r \sim \lambda_C = \hbar/(Mc)$.

  \;-\; \underbrace{\varepsilon\,\sigma_\mu\sigma_\nu}_{\text{Tier 1: Classical gravity}}

\section{Demoting the Metric: The $\sigma$-Field Equations}
\label{sec:demote}
%=============================================================

In general relativity, $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the fundamental dynamical variable and
Einstein's field equations are the equations of motion.  In QGD, we
\textbf{demote} the metric to a derived quantity:
\begin{equation}
  g_{\mu\nu} \;=\; g_{\mu\nu}[\sigma]
  \qquad\text{(metric is \emph{output}, not input)}
\end{equation}

The true dynamical variable is $\sigma_\mu(x)$.  Einstein's equations do not
disappear---they are recovered as the equilibrium limit of a deeper equation.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The configuration space}

The fundamental insight: the configuration space of QGD consists of sections
of a $\sigma$-bundle over spacetime.  The action is a functional of $\sigma$:
\begin{equation}
  S[\sigma] \;\equiv\; S\bigl[g(\sigma)\bigr]
  \label{eq:config_space}
\end{equation}
where $g_{\mu\nu}(\sigma)$ is the composite field given by
Eq.~\eqref{eq:master}.  The $\sigma$-field is a pre-geometric strain field;
$g_{\mu\nu}$ is the derived elastic response.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The complete action}

The unique diffeomorphism-invariant action at leading order is
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{S[\sigma] = \int\!\dd^4x\,\sqrt{-g(\sigma)}\left[
    \frac{R[g(\sigma)]}{16\pi G}
    + \frac{\hbar^2}{2M}\,g^{\mu\nu}\nabla_\mu\sigma^\alpha\nabla_\nu\sigma_\alpha
    + \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{matter}}(\psi,\,g(\sigma))
  \right]}
  \label{eq:action}
\end{equation}

Three terms:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Einstein--Hilbert in $\sigma$-variables.}
    The curvature scalar $R[g(\sigma)]$ is the unique second-order
    diffeomorphism-invariant scalar constructible from a metric.  We do not
    postulate GR; Einstein gravity \emph{emerges} because curvature is the
    only available geometric invariant.

  \item \textbf{The $\sigma$-kinetic term.}
    The term $(\hbar^2/2M)(\nabla\sigma)^2$ is \emph{not optional}.  It is
    forced by dimensional necessity ($\sigma$ is dimensionless; $\hbar^2/M$ is
    the only available scale), stability (without it $\sigma$ satisfies purely
    algebraic constraints), and its quantum origin from coarse-graining the
    Dirac phase.  It is the gravitational analogue of an elastic stiffness
    modulus.

  \item \textbf{Matter coupling.}
    The Dirac Lagrangian $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{matter}}$ couples to the
    \emph{derived} metric $g(\sigma)$, not to $\sigma$ directly.  This
    preserves the equivalence principle.
\end{enumerate}

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The $\sigma$-field equation}

The key technical step: vary with respect to $\sigma_\alpha$,
\textbf{not} $g_{\mu\nu}$.

\textbf{Chain rule variation:}
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\delta S}{\delta\sigma_\alpha}
  = \frac{\delta S}{\delta g_{\mu\nu}}\,
    \frac{\partial g_{\mu\nu}}{\partial\sigma_\alpha}
  - \frac{\hbar^2}{M}\,\nabla^2\sigma^\alpha
  = 0
  \label{eq:variation}
\end{equation}

Using the standard metric variation
$\delta S/\delta g_{\mu\nu} = \sqrt{-g}\,(G^{\mu\nu} - 8\pi G\,T^{\mu\nu})/(16\pi G)$,
this yields the fundamental dynamical equation of QGD:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\frac{\hbar^2}{M}\,\nabla^2\sigma^\alpha
  = \frac{1}{16\pi G}\left(G^{\mu\nu} - \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T^{\mu\nu}\right)
    \frac{\partial g_{\mu\nu}}{\partial\sigma_\alpha}}
  \label{eq:sigma_eom}
\end{equation}

\textbf{This equation does not exist in general relativity.}

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Recovery of Einstein's equations at equilibrium}

When $\nabla^2\sigma^\alpha = 0$ (static configuration), the left-hand side
vanishes and Eq.~\eqref{eq:sigma_eom} reduces to
\begin{equation}
  \left(G^{\mu\nu} - \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T^{\mu\nu}\right)
  \frac{\partial g_{\mu\nu}}{\partial\sigma_\alpha} = 0
  \label{eq:einstein_projection}
\end{equation}

Since $\partial g_{\mu\nu}/\partial\sigma_\alpha$ has rank~4 in $\sigma$-space,
this projects Einstein's equations onto the $\sigma$-manifold, recovering:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T_{\mu\nu}}
  \label{eq:EFE}
\end{equation}

The dichotomy is sharp:
\begin{equation}
  \begin{cases}
    \nabla^2\sigma = 0 &\longrightarrow\quad
      \text{Classical GR (static $\sigma$ configuration)} \\[4pt]
    \nabla^2\sigma \neq 0 &\longrightarrow\quad
      \text{Quantum gravity ($\sigma$ fluctuations)}
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}

GR is the equilibrium thermodynamics of the graviton field.  Quantum gravity
is its statistical mechanics.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Recursive structure}

Expanding $\sigma_\mu$ in the joint $(\hbar,\epsilon)$ expansion, each order
yields a \emph{linear} PDE sourced by lower-order fields:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\Box\,\sigma_\mu^{(n,k)}
  = S_\mu^{(n,k)}\!\left[\sigma^{(n'<n,\;k'<k)}\right]}
  \label{eq:recursive}
\end{equation}

Given boundary conditions, this uniquely determines $\sigma_\mu^{(n,k)}$ at
each order.  The $n = 0$ classical hierarchy reproduces the full
post-Newtonian expansion; the $n \geq 1$ terms provide quantum corrections at
each PN level.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Schwarzschild}

Spherically symmetric mass $M$, no rotation or charge.

\textbf{Step 1.}  Graviton field:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_t = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{c^2 r}}
    \left(1 + \frac{\hbar^2}{2M^2 c^2 r^2} + \cdots\right)
  \label{eq:sigma_schw}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Step 2.}  Graviton tensor:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_{tt} = \sigma_t^2
  = \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} + \frac{G\hbar^2}{Mc^4 r^3}

\end{equation}

\textbf{Step 3.}  Master equation with $\varepsilon = +1$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\dd s^2
  = \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} - \frac{G\hbar^2}{Mc^4 r^3}\right)c^2\,\dd t^2
  - \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} - \frac{G\hbar^2}{Mc^4 r^3}\right)^{\!-1}\dd r^2

  \label{eq:schwarzschild}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Classical limit} ($\hbar \to 0$): standard Schwarzschild.~$\checkmark$

Charged, non-rotating mass with charge $Q$.

\begin{equation}
  \Sigma^2 = \varepsilon_M\,(\sigma_t^{(M)})^2 + \varepsilon_Q\,(\sigma_t^{(Q)})^2
  = \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} - \frac{Gk_e^2 Q^2}{c^4 r^2}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Step 3.}
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\dd s^2
  = \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} + \frac{Gk_e^2 Q^2}{c^4 r^2}\right)c^2\,\dd t^2
  - \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{c^2 r} + \frac{Gk_e^2 Q^2}{c^4 r^2}\right)^{\!-1}\dd r^2
  - r^2\,\dd\Omega^2}
  \label{eq:rn}
\end{equation}

Standard Reissner--Nordstr\"om.~$\checkmark$

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Kerr}

Rotating mass with angular momentum $J = Mac$.

\textbf{Step 1.}  Two nonzero components:
\begin{align}
  \sigma_t &= \sqrt{\frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}},
    &\Sigma &= r^2 + a^2\cos^2\theta \\[4pt]
  \sigma_\phi &= \frac{a\sin\theta}{r}
    \sqrt{\frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}},
    &\varepsilon &= +1
\end{align}

Note: $\sigma_\phi$ does \emph{not} include the $r\sin\theta$ Jacobian
factor---that is carried by $T^\phi{}_\phi$.

\textbf{Step 2.}  Metric components:
\begin{align}
  g_{tt} &= 1 - \frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma} \\[4pt]
  g_{t\phi}^{(\mathrm{inner})}
    &= -\sigma_t\,\sigma_\phi \quad\xrightarrow{\;T\;}\quad
  g_{t\phi} = -r\sin\theta\;\sigma_t\;\sigma_\phi
  = -\frac{2GMra\sin^2\theta}{c^2\,\Sigma}
\end{align}

Frame-dragging emerges as the \emph{interference pattern} between
the temporal and azimuthal graviton waves.

\textbf{Step 3.}  Complete Kerr metric:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\begin{aligned}
    \dd s^2
    &= \left(1-\frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}\right)c^2\,\dd t^2
      - \frac{\Sigma}{\Delta}\,\dd r^2
      - \Sigma\,\dd\theta^2 \\[4pt]
    &\quad - \left(r^2+a^2+\frac{2GMra^2\sin^2\theta}{c^2\,\Sigma}\right)
      \sin^2\!\theta\;\dd\phi^2
      - \frac{4GMra\sin^2\theta}{c^2\,\Sigma}\;c\,\dd t\,\dd\phi
  \end{aligned}}
  \label{eq:kerr}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta = r^2 - 2GMr/c^2 + a^2$.~$\checkmark$

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Kerr--Newman}

Three graviton fields combine by superposition:
\begin{align}
  \sigma_\mu^{(M)} &= \left(\sqrt{\frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}},\;0,\;0,\;0\right),
    &\varepsilon_M &= +1 \\[2pt]
  \sigma_\mu^{(Q)} &= \left(\sqrt{\frac{Gk_e^2 Q^2}{c^4\,\Sigma}},\;0,\;0,\;0\right),
    &\varepsilon_Q &= -1 \\[2pt]
  \sigma_\mu^{(J)} &= \left(0,\;0,\;0,\;
    \frac{a\sin\theta}{r}\sqrt{\frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}}\right),
    &\varepsilon_J &= +1
\end{align}

The $g_{tt}$ component:
\begin{equation}
  g_{tt} = 1 - \frac{2GMr}{c^2\,\Sigma}
    + \frac{Gk_e^2 Q^2}{c^4\,\Sigma}
  = 1 - \frac{2GMr - Gk_e^2 Q^2/c^2}{\Sigma}
\end{equation}

This is \textbf{exactly} the Kerr--Newman metric, obtained purely from
algebraic combination of three graviton fields.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Schwarzschild--de Sitter}

Mass $M$ with positive cosmological constant.

\textbf{Step 1.}  Two sources:
\begin{align}
  \sigma_t^{(M)} &= \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{c^2 r}},
    &\varepsilon_M &= +1 \\[2pt]
  \sigma_t^{(\Lambda)} &= \frac{Hr}{c},
